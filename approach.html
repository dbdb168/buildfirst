How we think

/How We Think About AI Fluency

Building capability through use, not studying theory.

Philosophy

/Recalibration, Not Training

Most AI training treats fluency as knowledge transfer: learn the concepts, pass the quiz, apply the framework. We've found that doesn't work for leaders who need to make decisions under uncertainty. Real fluency comes from recalibrated instinct — the ability to see where AI creates genuine value, act on it confidently, and adjust when the tool falls short. That instinct only develops through building, failing, and refining in context.

Methodology

/Four Principles We Build On

01

Build Before You Teach

We start every engagement by building something useful together. Not a demo. Not a slide deck. A real workflow that solves an actual problem the leader or team faces today. The learning happens inside the building.

02

Diagnose First, Prescribe Second

We don't assume we know what you need. Every engagement begins with understanding your specific workflows, constraints, and organizational context. The right AI application emerges from that diagnosis, not from a generic playbook.

03

Strategic Overlay on Every Build

When we build custom tools, agents, or workflows, the strategic thinking isn't separate from the technical work. We come in at the strategic level first, then deliver working software that embodies the insight. The build is the deliverable, but the thinking is what makes it valuable.

04

Fluency Is Personal Before It's Organizational

Team fluency starts with individual confidence. Leaders who have built real AI workflows themselves make better decisions about team adoption, tool selection, and investment. That's why 1:1 coaching is often the front door to broader organizational work.

Framework

/The Anthropic Fluency Framework

Our methodology draws on the Anthropic fluency framework, which defines AI fluency across four dimensions: prompting, evaluating outputs, integrating AI into workflows, and understanding limitations. We adapt this framework for executives and teams who need practical capability, not academic mastery. The goal isn't to become an AI expert. It's to become a more effective leader in an AI-shaped world.
