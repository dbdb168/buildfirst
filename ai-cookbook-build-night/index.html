How this started

A few months ago, on a Friday afternoon, I had an idea for something I wanted to show some friends. I sat down with AI and described what I wanted. An hour later — glass of wine in hand — I was showing them a working prototype.

I didn't realize what I'd done was special until one of them said: "Wait — you built this? In an hour?"

That was the moment I realized: if I can do this, anyone can. I'm not a developer. I'm a 52-year-old dad of two who spent his career in design thinking and innovation — at IDEO, Capital One, DBS Bank — and stumbled into building with AI because I was curious.

Since then, I've built about 80 things. Most of them I don't use anymore — and that's the point. The joy is in the building. The play. The surprise when something works that you didn't think was possible.

The things I kept? They give me more time with my kids, not less. That's the whole point. Build the tools that handle the logistics, so you can be present for the stuff that matters.

Build Night is me giving you that same Friday-evening moment. One evening, one build, and a completely different way of seeing what's possible.

No. Building with AI is more like having a conversation than writing code. You describe what you want in plain English, and AI helps you build it. David isn't a developer either — he learned by doing, which is exactly what you'll do.
Is this better as a couple or can I come solo?
Both work great. Couples tend to build different things and then compare notes — it's one of the best parts. But plenty of people come on their own and have a brilliant time. Each person registers separately and needs their own laptop.
What's Claude Pro and why do I need it?
Claude is an AI tool made by Anthropic — it's what we'll use to build. The Pro plan ($20/month) gives you access to the best model available, which is what makes the builds work well. I make no money from this — it's simply the tool I use myself and trust the most. We'll send you step-by-step setup instructions after you register.

That's exactly who this is for. Being bad at tech is actually freeing here — there's nothing to unlearn. The most interesting builds often come from people who've never done anything like this before. Small group means nobody gets left behind.
Will this actually be useful, or is it a novelty?
You'll leave with something you'll use this week. Not a party trick — a tool that solves a real problem in your family life. But honestly, the bigger thing you walk away with is confidence. The fear of AI goes away when you've built something with it.

Fair concern. The whole point of what we build is to give you more time with the people you love — not less. The tools handle the logistics so you don't have to. Build it once, and it works quietly in the background while you're present for the stuff that matters.
